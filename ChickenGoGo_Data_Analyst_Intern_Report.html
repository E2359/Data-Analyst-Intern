8. Data Quality and Limitations

  • The sales data covers a short period, so conclusions should be treated as early signals, not long-term trends.
  • Item-level sales are not included, so menu analysis is limited to price comparison rather than true product profitability.
  • Some payroll rows may represent fixed half-month pay instead of hourly labor, so those rows should not be mixed with normal hourly employees without labeling.
  • Sales and time clock dates do not fully overlap in the current cleaned files, so sales per labor hour should be improved after collecting matching dates.
  • Supplier price comparison should consider unit size, product quality, delivery fees, and availability before switching vendors.
